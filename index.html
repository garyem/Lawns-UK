Clay Soil Lawn Care Schedule – UK

By: GaryM

Looking after a lawn in the UK — especially on clay soil — takes a little extra love, but the rewards are well worth it. Clay holds onto nutrients and moisture better than sandy soils, which means your lawn can thrive with the right care. Regular aeration and seasonal scarifying help relieve compaction, while top-dressing with compost improves structure and encourages deep, healthy roots. For a more sustainable approach, choose organic lawn feeds, compost grass clippings, and water early in the morning to reduce evaporation. By working with the natural properties of clay and embracing eco-friendly methods, you’ll build a greener, more resilient lawn that supports biodiversity — and looks brilliant year-round.

Season Goals Recommended Actions
Spring (Mar–May) Wake up the lawn, relieve compaction, boost healthy growth
  • Scarify (Mar/Apr)
  • Aerate (Apr)
  • Feed with Mo Bacter or Westland SafeLawn
  • Optional: top-dress with loam/sand/compost mix
Summer (Jun–Aug) Maintain health, resist drought and wear
  • Light feeding (low nitrogen)
  • Water deeply in early mornings
  • Mow at 3–4 cm height, sharpen blades
Autumn (Sep–Oct) Repair and strengthen before winter
  • Scarify and aerate again
  • Overseed with fescue/rye mix
  • Feed with EverGreen Autumn Lawn Care
  • Light top-dressing
Winter (Nov–Feb) Let the lawn rest and recover
  • Avoid walking on frosty/waterlogged grass
  • Clear fallen leaves
  • No feeding required
